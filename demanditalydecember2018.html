Electricity demand in Italy, December 2018

Terna S.p.A. recently released numbers from the month of December

Decrease in demand

In the month of December 2018, according to what Terna (the company that manages the italian electricity grid) detected, the demand for electricity was 26.5 TWh, dereased by 1.5% compared to the same month of 2017. The trend of the demand suffered because of calendarial and temperature effects. There was one more working day in 2018 (19 vs 18), but the average temperature of December was on degree celsius higher than that of the year before. The seasonally adjusted data corrected with temperature and calendar, leads to a variation of electricity demand down by 1.1%.

More Renewable

According to the elaborated data from Terna, the toal electricity request in Italy in 2018 amounts to 321.9 TWh, a value slightly increased (0.4%) compared to 2017 (variation does not change with the same calendar). The demand for electricity in 2018, which was made up of 35% renewable energy production (compared to 32% in 2017) respresents the highest value since 2013. The electricity demand was lower everywhere; -1.5% in the north and the south, and -1.3% in the centre of Italy.

Compared to the month of November it was registered an increase of 0.4% of demand in December adjusted to seasonal variations, temperature and working calendar, but the trend profile in December followed a weakly decrease.

In the month of December 2018, the demand of electricity in Italy was compromised of 86.6% with national production while the remaining 13.4% was traded with neighbouring countries. The national production was 23.2 TWh a decrease of 2.8% compared to December 2017. Hydro power production increased 56.7%, and solar increased 5.8%. Geothermal decreased 1.2%, thermal 9.2% and wind power production decreased 15.4%.
